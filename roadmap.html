3-Month AI Accessibility Roadmap

Based on user testing with 5 small business owners · Published March 2026

SimpleAI's mission is to make AI genuinely useful for people who weren't at the table when it was designed — small business owners, service workers, and non-technical operators who have real problems and no time to learn new tools. This roadmap translates direct user feedback into the three barriers we're removing next.

Target persona
Non-technical small business owners in service industries
Current tool
simpleai-nine.vercel.app — AI reply generator for customer emails & reviews
Evidence base
5 user test sessions · March 2026 (full report)
Month 1
Direct Platform Connections
April 2026
Reduce friction
AI barrier being removed
"I have to switch tabs, find the message, copy it, come back here, paste it. By the time I do all that I've lost the thread of what I was doing."
Features shipping this month
  • ▸ Google Reviews connector — paste a Google review URL and the tool fetches the review text automatically. No copy-paste required.
  • ▸ Gmail quick-paste button — browser bookmark that extracts the selected email text and opens SimpleAI pre-filled.
  • ▸ Mobile layout overhaul — responsive redesign so the tool works without frustration on a phone, where most small business owners read reviews.
User testing evidence

Month 2
Brand Voice Learning
May 2026
Build trust
AI barrier being removed
"The replies don't sound like me. They sound like a corporate HR department. My customers know how I talk — this would fool nobody."
Features shipping this month
  • ▸ Voice profile setup — users paste 2–3 replies they've written before. The AI extracts vocabulary, sentence length, and warmth level to build a reusable style profile.
  • ▸ Saved profile persistence — profile is stored locally (no signup required). Next visit, it loads automatically.
  • ▸ "Sounds like me" rating — a dedicated feedback signal (separate from helpful/not helpful) so we can measure voice accuracy over time.
User testing evidence
"It sounded like me — warm, genuine, not corporate. I'd just add my name at the bottom. — Tester B (positive outlier — most testers felt the opposite)
"It's too generic. It doesn't mention food, the experience, nothing specific to a restaurant. — Tester C

Month 3
Reply History & Smart Templates
June 2026
Build confidence
AI barrier being removed
"I don't trust it to get it right every time. What if it says something wrong and I don't catch it? I need to feel in control."
Features shipping this month
  • ▸ Reply history — the last 20 replies a user generated are saved locally. They can review, copy, or reuse them without regenerating.
  • ▸ Save as template — star any reply to save it as a reusable template for common situations (e.g. "late order apology", "5-star thank you").
  • ▸ Inline editing — edit the generated reply directly in the output box before copying. Changes are preserved if you regenerate.
  • ▸ Situation prompts — pre-filled scenario buttons (Late delivery, Wrong order, Damaged item, Great service) that auto-populate the message field to remove the blank-page anxiety.
User testing evidence
"Could you let me regenerate with one click without clearing everything? Sometimes the first draft is close but not quite there. — Tester E
"I'd use it as a draft starter. The structure is good — I just need to add the specifics. — Tester C
